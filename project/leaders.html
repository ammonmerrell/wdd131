Civilizations and Leaders

Introduction to Leaders

Each leader has a unique ability that helps some aspect of the game. You continue to play as the same leader for all three "eras" or sections of the game, meaning you will keep the ability throughout the game.

Augustus: can buy buildings cheaper in towns and can buy culture buildings in towns.

(B)Charlemagne: recieve two free calvary units when a clebration happens, and recieve happines for each quarter next to millitary and science buildings.

(B)Benjamin Franklin: can build prodution buildings faster, and recieve science from production buildings.

Introduction to Civilizations

Civilizations have a unique ability, two unique buildings/unique inprovement, associated wonder, unique civics, millitary unit, and civillian unit. You change civilizations (civ) every era, meaning you change the related bonuses three times over a game.

Antiquity Age

(B)Rome: recieve 1 culture for each district in the capital, and 1 culture for each town hall.

Greece: starts with extra influence in the capital

(B)Han: the capital and towns get one extra population when they grow.

Exploration Age

(B)Norman: land units next to coast are stronger and embarked land units move further.

Abbasid: recieve gold for each rural of a city when you create a specialist.

spain: cheaper cost to convert towns to cities, increased bonus in distant lands.

Modern Age

(B)Mexico: unique clebration effect which gives extra culture.

Siam: has a unique action to instantly become a leader of a City-State.

America: gain gold when you inprove a resourse.

Detailed breakdown

(B)Rome: Rome is good at generating culture and having a millitary. The AI players may not like you and that can sometimes be out of your control, so it can be good to have a civ skilled at creating an army to defend yourself.

(B)Norman: The normans are a defensive civ with the most unique civics in the game at five. They can also be good at attacking over water in the highly contested distant lands.

(B)Mexico: Mexico may be the most versitile civ in the game. They can have a strong millitary, happines yeild, and science and culture yeild, they can do it all. All you need to do is slot your unique traditions in to unlock the potential of Mexico.
